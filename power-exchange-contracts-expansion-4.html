The Queen's Voice — Sacred Listening

The devoted acknowledges that Her voice, delivered through headphones, is the primary vessel of this dynamic. In the absence of physical presence, voice becomes body — it carries warmth, authority, tenderness, and command. The devoted shall treat every listening session as sacred.

Before each listening session, the devoted shall create conditions of stillness and receptivity: a quiet room, comfortable and dignified posture, headphones positioned, distractions silenced. The devoted shall not listen to the Queen's voice while driving, working, or dividing attention. When She speaks, the world shall stop — or the devoted shall wait until it can.

The Queen's recorded assignments — mantras to memorise, instructions to follow, reflections to absorb — shall be listened to in full, with focus, as many times as the Queen directs. Partial attention is not devotion. Background listening is not reverence. The devoted shall offer the Queen the full cathedral of their mind.

The agreement may be renewed for successive ninety-day terms, modified by mutual agreement, or concluded with honour. Either party may request an interim review at any time.

Consent — The Vulnerability of Distance

Distance may amplify vulnerability. Without the grounding of physical presence, emotions may escalate, uncertainty may deepen, and the devoted may feel the weight of absence more keenly than expected. The devoted retains the absolute right to request additional contact, reassurance, or adjustment at any time. The need for closeness is not weakness — it is the honest pulse of a heart that has offered itself across the miles. The Queen shall receive all such requests with care, recognising that the strength of this dynamic is measured not by how much the devoted can endure in isolation, but by how honestly the devoted can ask for what they need.

The ceremony closes when the Queen dismisses the devoted. Signed copies shall be retained by each party.

The Principle of Specific Consent
  1. No modification shall occur without specific, documented consent for that individual modification. This agreement establishes the framework — it does not constitute blanket consent. Each mark, each piercing, each alteration requires its own moment of willing, informed, uncoerced agreement.
  2. This agreement is itself revocable at any time. The Canvas may close the modification chapter permanently with a single spoken or written declaration, and all outstanding or planned modifications shall be immediately cancelled without question, justification, or consequence.
  3. The Canvas retains an absolute and immediate veto over any proposed design, at any stage — from initial concept to the moment before the needle touches skin. No explanation is required. No justification is owed. The veto is final and shall be honoured without discussion.
  4. No modification shall proceed under pressure, coercion, guilt, or altered mental state. The Queen shall ensure that each consent is given in sobriety, clarity, and genuine willingness. Consent obtained under duress — emotional, psychological, or circumstantial — is void.
Categories of Modification
The following categories fall within the scope of this agreement, subject to the principle of specific consent above:
  1. Tattooing — Permanent or semi-permanent ink applied to the skin by a licensed tattoo artist, conveying designs, symbols, words, or marks as the Queen directs and the Canvas consents to.
  2. Piercing — The insertion of jewellery through the skin or cartilage by a licensed professional, in locations and with jewellery as designated by the Queen and consented to by the Canvas.
  3. Branding — The application of controlled heat to create a permanent scar in a deliberate design, performed only by an experienced, qualified practitioner under sanitary conditions.
  4. Scarification — The deliberate creation of raised or textured scars through cutting or other controlled methods, performed only by a qualified practitioner with full informed consent regarding permanence and aesthetic outcome.
  5. Temporary marking — Henna, body paint, temporary tattoos, or other non-permanent marks that convey devotion for a designated period before fading. These may serve as trials for permanent modifications or as standalone rituals.
  6. Grooming & presentation mandates — Directives governing hair length and style, body hair, nail presentation, or other aspects of physical presentation that the Queen may prescribe. These are reversible modifications and may be adjusted at any time.
