This scene works best with deliberate pacing. Let silences stretch. The power is in the waiting, not the words. If your subject shows signs of deep subspace, transition into aftercare immediately — this script can drop someone fast.

Queen
Queen

Prisoner
Prisoner

Scene Notes

The interrogation works best when the "crime" is real — a small rule broken, a task forgotten. The power comes from genuine confession. Stay cold but never cruel. If the prisoner becomes genuinely distressed, break character: "Pause. I'm here. You're safe." Scene intensity must serve connection, not damage it.

Queen

Worshipper

Queen
Queen

Queen

Close your eyes. Let them work. Let yourself receive.
Queen
"My hands next. Each finger. The palm. The back. Show me what my hands mean to you."
Queen
"These hands guide you. Correct you. Reward you. They deserve your full attention."
Queen

Queen

Queen
"I accept your worship. You may rest your head on my knee now."

Scene Notes

The worship scene is about receiving. Let yourself be worshipped without guilt. You deserve this. The worshipper's reward is your pleasure at being venerated. If receiving is difficult for you, start with five minutes and build tolerance over time. Receiving is a skill.

Queen

Offender

Offender

Queen
Queen

Queen

Queen
"This is the real correction — not the pain, but the forgiveness after. Do you feel it?"
Offender
Offender

Scene Notes

Punishment must always end with reconnection. The corrective act is not complete until the offender is held, forgiven, and reintegrated. Never leave someone in the punished state. The arc is: infraction → confession → consequence → forgiveness → closeness. Every time.

Scene Notes

This is the most emotionally significant scene in this collection. Do not perform it casually. The collaring should mark a genuine milestone in your dynamic — not a first-date activity. The vows should be personal and real. If you use a physical collar, choose one that can be worn comfortably and removed safely. The symbolism is in the commitment, not the hardware.
