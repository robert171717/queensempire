Purpose & Intention

Devotion is not instinct alone — it is cultivated, refined, and earned. With willing heart and open mind, I enter a formal period of training under Queen Elyria, acknowledging that the path of the devoted is one of deliberate growth. I do not arrive perfected; I arrive willing to be shaped.

It is agreed that through structured curriculum, defined milestones, and patient guidance, the trainee shall develop the skills, discipline, and inner architecture worthy of deeper service. This contract is not merely instruction — it is a path of transformation, each milestone a threshold crossed on the journey toward fuller surrender.

· · ·
Training Period & Structure
  1. The trainee shall enter a formal training period of ninety (90) days, commencing upon the date of signing. This period is divided into three thirty-day phases, each with distinct focus, assessment, and developmental goals.
  2. Phase I — Foundation (Days 1–30): The trainee shall learn the fundamental positions, protocols, rhythms, and expectations of the Queen's Empire. This phase establishes the bedrock upon which all further training is built.
  3. Phase II — Refinement (Days 31–60): The trainee shall deepen their practice, developing consistency, grace under pressure, and the capacity for anticipatory service. Correction becomes subtler; expectations rise.
  4. Phase III — Integration (Days 61–90): The trainee shall demonstrate mastery of core competencies, integrate training into instinctive behaviour, and prepare for graduation assessment. The devoted ceases to practise protocol and begins to embody it.

Assessment, Graduation & Remediation
  1. Assessment criteria shall include consistency of practice, quality of service, attitude and receptiveness to correction, and demonstrated growth across all modules. The Queen's assessment is comprehensive and Her judgement final.
  2. Graduation requirements: The trainee shall demonstrate competent mastery of all core modules, consistent adherence to protocols, and the maturity of devotion expected of one who has completed formal training. Graduation is earned, not assumed.
  3. Upon graduation, the trainee may be invited to enter deeper contracts within the Empire — advanced service agreements, expanded authority arrangements, or specialised roles as the Queen deems fitting. Graduation is not an end but a threshold.
  4. Remediation: Should milestones not be met, the Queen may extend a training phase, assign additional exercises, adjust the curriculum, or repeat a module. Remediation is guidance, not failure — the path bends but does not break.
  5. The trainee shall maintain a training journal, recording reflections, progress, difficulties, and insights. This journal shall be reviewed by the Queen at each checkpoint and serves as a record of the journey.
Duration & Renewal

This contract shall remain in effect for the ninety (90) day training period, commencing upon signing. Upon graduation or at the contract's conclusion, the parties shall review the experience and determine next steps — renewal for advanced training, advancement to deeper agreements, or conclusion with honour. Either party may request an interim review at any time.

Consent & the Trainee's Pace

Training proceeds at the pace the trainee can sustain with growth. Physical and emotional safety shall always override training goals — no milestone, no standard, no expectation is worth lasting harm. The trainee retains the absolute right to communicate overwhelm, request pacing adjustments, or pause training at any time. The Queen shall receive all such communications with care and adjust the curriculum accordingly. True mastery is built upon a foundation of trust, never upon erosion of the self.

Protocol & Etiquette Manual Addendum

Agreement XI of XII
Dated this _____ day of _______________, 20____
Sovereign: Queen Elyria, whose standards of conduct govern the Empire
Practitioner: _____________________________, who commits to the art of reverent conduct
Purpose & Intention

Protocol is the language through which devotion is spoken without a single word. The angle of a bow, the cadence of a greeting, the stillness held in a crowded room — each detail is a sentence in the ongoing letter of reverence. This manual exists to refine that language into fluency.

This addendum expands upon Agreement IV (Ritual & Protocol), providing the detailed etiquette that governs the practitioner's conduct across every context of service. It is incorporated by reference into that agreement and shall be treated as its living extension — a rulebook for the art of presence, respect, and devotion.

· · ·
Forms of Address
The practitioner shall employ the correct form of address for each context:
  1. "My Queen" — for intimate, private, and deeply personal moments. This is the tender form, spoken in closeness, offered in vulnerability.
  2. "Queen Elyria" — for formal address, structured conversation, and public settings where dignity and propriety are paramount.
  3. "Your Majesty" / "My Sovereign" — for ritual contexts, ceremonial moments, and occasions of heightened reverence, as designated by the Queen.
  4. The practitioner shall not address the Queen by first name, diminutive, or casual form unless explicit permission has been granted for a specific context. Even then, such permission is contextual, not universal.
  5. The practitioner shall greet the Queen upon entering Her presence and offer a farewell upon departure, using the appropriate form. No encounter begins or ends without acknowledgment.
Speaking Protocols
  1. The practitioner shall request permission before speaking at length, introducing new topics, or asking questions beyond simple logistics. The phrase "My Queen, may I speak?" or the Queen's designated equivalent shall precede substantive address.
  2. When granted permission to speak, the practitioner shall be concise, honest, and measured. Rambling, evasion, or excessive self-reference shall be corrected.
  3. The practitioner shall not interrupt the Queen under any circumstance. If something urgent must be communicated, the designated signal — a raised hand, a specific word, or a physical gesture — shall be used, and the Queen shall acknowledge in Her own time.
  4. Disagreement, when necessary, shall be expressed with deference: "With respect, my Queen, may I offer a perspective?" The Queen's decision following any such offering is final.
  5. The practitioner shall refer to themselves in the third person or by the Queen's designated term when in formal protocol mode, as directed: ____________________
Table & Service Protocol
  1. When serving food or drink, the practitioner shall present from the Queen's left side, set items down gently and precisely, and withdraw without turning their back until at appropriate distance. Service is silent, fluid, and invisible — noticed only by its perfection.
  2. The practitioner shall not begin eating, drinking, or sitting until the Queen has been served and has given explicit permission. The Queen eats first, always.
  3. The practitioner shall anticipate refills, clear promptly, and maintain the serving space with immaculate attention. No empty glass, no cluttered surface, no unmet need shall persist in the practitioner's presence.
  4. When dining in the Queen's company, the practitioner shall eat with composure, maintain posture, and engage in conversation only when addressed or permitted. Utensils, posture, and pace shall reflect the standard the Queen establishes.
Gift Protocol
  1. Gifts to the Queen are expressions of devotion, not transactions. The practitioner shall offer gifts that reflect attentiveness to the Queen's tastes, interests, and aesthetic — never gifts that presume familiarity or seek to obligate.
  2. Appropriate gifts may include: items reflecting the Queen's known preferences, handwritten correspondence, tokens of symbolic meaning, or offerings designated by the Queen. Specific guidance: ____________________
  3. Presentation: Gifts shall be presented on both palms, head bowed, with a spoken offering appropriate to the occasion. The practitioner shall not expect a specific reaction and shall receive whatever response the Queen gives with grace.
  4. Occasions: The practitioner may offer gifts on designated occasions — anniversaries, milestones, holidays — or spontaneously as devotion moves them. The Queen's acceptance is itself a gift.
Punishment Acceptance Ritual
  1. When correction is administered, the practitioner shall assume the designated punishment position — kneeling, head lowered, hands in the prescribed placement — and remain still until addressed.
  2. The practitioner shall receive correction in silence unless instructed to speak. When permitted, the words of acceptance shall be spoken: "I receive your correction, my Queen, with gratitude." Gratitude is not for pain but for the Queen's attention and investment in growth.
  3. The practitioner shall not argue, justify, or deflect during correction. If the practitioner believes a correction is unwarranted, this shall be raised privately and respectfully after the correction has been received and the moment has passed.
  4. Following correction, the practitioner shall offer a reflection — written or spoken, as designated — acknowledging the lesson, expressing renewed commitment, and closing the matter with intention.
Public & Private Conduct
  1. In private: Full protocol applies. The practitioner shall maintain positions, address forms, service expectations, and behavioural standards as established throughout this manual.
  2. In public: Protocol adapts to context. The practitioner shall maintain composure, deference, and attentiveness in ways appropriate to the setting — subtle to the untrained eye, unmistakable to those who understand. The Queen shall designate the appropriate protocol level for each public context.
  3. At events: The practitioner shall remain attentive to the Queen's needs, maintain position as designated (at Her side, in attendance, or in waiting), and conduct themselves as a reflection of the Empire's standards. Social interaction with others shall be gracious, brief, and never at the expense of attentiveness to the Queen.
  4. When meeting other devoted ones of the Empire, the practitioner shall conduct themselves with mutual respect, recognise the hierarchy the Queen has established, and never speak on behalf of, compare, or compete with fellow practitioners. All such interactions shall be governed by the Queen's designated protocols: ____________________
Duration & Incorporation

This addendum shall remain in continuous effect and is incorporated by reference into Agreement IV (Ritual & Protocol). It shall be treated as the detailed expansion of that agreement's protocols. The Queen may amend, add, or retire specific etiquette standards at Her discretion. A formal review shall be conducted every sixty (60) days.

Consent & Negotiation

This manual is entered into with the understanding that protocol exists to deepen connection, not to constrain the spirit. Any standard that causes genuine distress or feels inconsistent with the practitioner's wellbeing shall be communicated openly, and the Queen shall adjust with care. Etiquette is the servant of devotion — never its master.

Covenant of Sensory & Information Control

Agreement XII of XII
Dated this _____ day of _______________, 20____
Curator: Queen Elyria, who curates what the recipient sees, hears, reads, and consumes
Recipient: _____________________________, who willingly surrenders the curation of their inner world
Purpose & Intention

What we consume shapes what we become. The thoughts we entertain, the images we absorb, the voices we permit into our inner world — these become the architecture of the mind. With full willingness, I offer Queen Elyria the authority to curate that architecture, trusting that Her vision for my inner life is more intentional than my own unguided wandering.

It is agreed that through the surrender of autonomous consumption, the recipient shall find a mind that is quieter, more focused, and more attuned to devotion. The noise of the world recedes; the Queen's voice becomes the clearest signal. This covenant is for the deeply surrendered — those who understand that the deepest power exchange encompasses not only the body and the will, but the mind itself.

· · ·
Media Protocol
  1. The following categories of media consumption require the Queen's explicit prior permission: social media browsing or posting, news and current events consumption, adult or erotic content, entertainment media (films, television, streaming, gaming), podcasts and video content, and any content the Queen has designated as restricted.
  2. Forbidden content categories — the following are excluded from consumption under all circumstances, as designated by the Queen: ______________________________
  3. Permitted content categories — the following may be consumed without individual approval, as designated by the Queen: ______________________________
  4. The recipient shall not consume media in secret, in private without disclosure, or through means intended to circumvent this covenant. All consumption is transparent.
Reading & Listening Assignments
  1. The Queen may assign specific reading materials, audio content, or studies for the recipient to complete. These assignments are curated to shape the recipient's mind toward focus, devotion, growth, or perspective as the Queen intends.
  2. The recipient shall complete all assigned materials within the timeframe designated and offer a reflection — written or spoken — on what was absorbed. Reflection demonstrates not merely consumption but integration.
  3. The Queen may designate a daily or weekly reading/listening schedule as the recipient's mental nourishment. This curated diet replaces unguided consumption as the primary source of intellectual and emotional input.
Digital Device Protocol
  1. The recipient's digital devices — phone, computer, tablet — shall be used only during designated periods and for designated purposes, as the Queen directs. Specific permitted usage windows: ______________________________
  2. The Queen shall determine which applications, platforms, and digital spaces are permitted. The recipient shall not access restricted applications or create accounts on platforms not approved.
  3. Device-free periods: The Queen may designate periods during which all devices are surrendered, stored, or powered down. These periods of digital silence are opportunities for presence, reflection, and connection to the immediate.
  4. The recipient shall not delete, hide, or obscure browsing history, message logs, or consumption records. The Queen retains the right to inspect the recipient's digital activity at any time, and the recipient shall maintain all records with transparency.
Information Fasting & Sensory Direction
  1. Information fasts: The Queen may designate periods — hours, days, or longer — during which the recipient consumes no external information whatsoever. No news, no social media, no entertainment. These fasts clear the mind and create space for the Queen's voice to be heard most clearly.
  2. Sensory enrichment: Conversely, the Queen may direct specific sensory input — music, ambient sound, visual environments, scents — to shape the recipient's inner atmosphere. The recipient shall accept and immerse in these environments as directed.
  3. Sensory restriction: The Queen may, within negotiated limits, direct periods of sensory reduction — blindfolds, earplugs, silence, stillness — as practice in presence, patience, and surrender to the unknown. These practices deepen the recipient's capacity to exist within the Queen's will without the distraction of autonomous input.
  4. The recipient shall report the effects of all sensory direction — mental state, emotional response, physical sensation — honestly and in detail. This information guides the Queen's ongoing curation.
Duration & Renewal

This covenant shall remain in effect for an initial period of sixty (60) days, commencing upon signing. A thorough review shall be conducted at the end of this period to assess the recipient's experience, mental and emotional wellbeing, and the effects of the curated environment. The covenant may be renewed, adjusted, or concluded by mutual decision.

· · ·
Consent — Psychological Edge Play
Sacred Carve-Out — Emergency & Essential Access

This covenant governs the recipient's voluntary media and information environment. It does not govern and shall never restrict the recipient's access to: emergency communications and services, medical and health information, communications from family and dependents, legal or financial notifications, or any information necessary for the recipient's safety, rights, or responsibilities. These channels remain accessible at all times, without permission, without delay, without exception.

This covenant is recognised as psychological edge play. The surrender of one's mental environment is among the most intimate forms of power exchange, and its effects can be profound. The recipient retains the absolute right to communicate overwhelm, mental fatigue, or the need for re-expansion of access at any time. The Covenant of Safety (Agreement V) — consent, limits, safewords, and check-in requirements — governs this covenant absolutely. The Queen shall exercise Her curatorial authority with wisdom, monitoring the recipient's mental and emotional state with care, and adjusting the environment to serve growth rather than erosion. A curated mind is a gift offered — never a prison imposed.
