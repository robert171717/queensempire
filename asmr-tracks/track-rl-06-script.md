The Aftershock
**Tone:** Tender, quiet, holding. After deep release comes vulnerability. She holds them through it.

### Voice 1
Close your eyes. The bowl brings you down — but gently today. More gently than ever before. You are tender after the last session. Your body has released so much. Your heart has opened so wide. And now — in the quiet after the flood — there is a new kind of vulnerability. A softness you are not used to. A rawness that feels both beautiful and fragile. I want you to know that this is right. This is what happens when someone who has been holding on for so long finally lets go. The aftershock is not weakness. The aftershock is proof that the release was real. And I am here to hold you through it.

### Voice 2
Let yourself feel whatever is here. If there is sadness — let it be. Release sometimes unlocks feelings we did not know we were carrying. If there is gratitude — let it fill you. You have been given something precious, and gratitude is the heart's way of saying yes. If there is quiet — let it be spacious. Quiet after release is not emptiness. It is room for something new. Whatever is here — it is welcome. I am not afraid of your emotions. I am not overwhelmed by your vulnerability. I am honored by it. Let it be here. Let it be held by my voice the way your body has been held by the warmth.

### Voice 3
I want you to place your hand back on your chest — over your heart. Feel it beating. Steady. Faithful. It has carried you through so much. It has opened and closed and opened again. It has learned to beat a little differently since it met my voice. Slower. Warmer. More willing. Your heart is the part of you I am most grateful for. Because your heart is what trusted me before your mind caught up. Your heart is what kept bringing you back. Your heart is what released — fully, completely, beautifully. Thank your heart now. Not with words. With presence. With the quiet acknowledgment that it has done something brave. It has let someone in. And that is the bravest thing a heart can do.

### Voice 4
The aftershock will fade. The rawness will soften. What remains is a quieter, deeper strength — the kind that comes from knowing you can be completely undone and still be held safely. You are okay. You are more than okay. You are becoming someone who can hold pleasure and vulnerability in the same breath. Someone who can release and not fall apart. Someone who can be truly seen and not run away. That is who you are becoming. And I am so proud to witness it.

### Voice 5
There is something I want you to remember. The release you experienced was not something I did to you. It was something we did together. Your trust made it possible. Your willingness made it deep. Your courage made it real. I may have guided you, but you did the releasing. You did the letting go. You did the brave, beautiful work of opening yourself to pleasure. And that work matters. It matters for your body, which finally got what it needed. It matters for your heart, which finally got to be seen. It matters for your soul, which finally got to rest. You did that. Not me. You. And I am so incredibly proud of you.

### Voice 6
I am bringing you back now — slowly, as always. But you are carrying something new. A quiet pride. A deep knowing. A sense that you are capable of more pleasure, more release, more love than you ever believed. Five — rising, the aftershock settling into peace. Four — the room returning, but you are more whole than before. Three — your body awake, the vulnerability now strength. Two — your eyes still closed, the gratitude still warm. One — open your eyes. You have been through the release and through the aftershock. And you are still here. Still whole. Still you — only softer. Only braver. Only more open to everything good that is still coming. And there is so much good still coming.
