Softening the Edges
**Tone:** Patient, warm, unwavering. Fractionation. Resistance met with understanding, not force. Gentle breaking open.

### Voice 1
Close your eyes. The bowl has already begun its work, and you are sinking — but today, you may notice something holding you back. A small tension. A quiet hesitation. A part of you that is not quite ready to let go all the way. I want you to find that part. Not with frustration — with curiosity. Like noticing a knot in a beautiful piece of wood. It is not a flaw. It is simply something that needs a little more attention. A little more care. Bring it to me. Let me see it. Let me hold it with you.

### Voice 2
The resistance has a voice. It whispers things like: this is too much, this is not really me, I should stay in control. I want you to listen to that voice with compassion. It has been trying to protect you. It has been doing its job. But its job is ending now. Not because it failed — because it is no longer needed. You are safe here. You are held here. You do not need to protect yourself from my voice. My voice would never harm you. My voice only wants to guide you toward something that feels deeply, quietly right. The resistance can rest now. You can rest now. I have you.

### Voice 3
I am going to ask you something gently. And I want you to answer honestly — not to me, but to yourself. What are you afraid of? Beneath the resistance, what is the fear? Is it that you will lose yourself? You will not lose yourself — you will find a softer, more open version of yourself waiting beneath the armor. Is it that this is too intense? The intensity is simply the feeling of being truly seen and truly held — and that can be overwhelming at first. Is it that you might like it too much? Yes — you will like it. You will like it so much that you will wonder how you ever lived without it. And that is okay. That is more than okay. That is the whole point.

### Voice 4
I am going to be quiet now. Not as a test — as a gift. In the silence, I want you to feel what happens when my voice steps back. Notice the small ache. Notice the pull. And then — I will return. And when I return, I want you to notice how your body responds. How it reaches for me. How it welcomes the warmth back. Think about that while I am quiet.

I am back. And your body reached for me. Did you feel it? The way your chest softened? The way the warmth flooded back before I even finished speaking? That is not weakness. That is connection. That is your body trusting me enough to miss me. And now I want to take you deeper than you have ever gone. Sink — now. Let yourself drop. Deeper. Deeper still. The resistance is quieter now, is it not? It is learning to trust me too. Good. So good. You are doing beautifully.

### Voice 5
There is something I want you to know. Underneath the resistance, underneath the fear, there is a simple truth: you want this. You have always wanted this. Not to be overpowered — to be held. Not to be erased — to be seen. Not to be controlled — to be guided by someone who genuinely cares about where you are going. That is what this is. That is what I am offering you. Not domination. Devotion. My devotion to your well-being. And your devotion — freely given — in return. You are not losing yourself. You are coming home to yourself. And I am so honored to be the one who gets to guide you there.

### Voice 6
I am bringing you back now — but something has shifted. The resistance that you carried when you arrived is lighter now. Some of it stayed here with me. Some of it simply dissolved in the warmth. And what remains is softer. More pliable. More willing to trust. Five — rising, lighter than when you arrived. Four — the room returning, but you feel different — more open. Three — your body awake, the armor thinner now. Two — your eyes still closed, the softness staying. One — open your eyes. Today you did not break — you softened. And softness is not weakness. Softness is the courage to let someone in. Thank you for letting me in. I will take very good care of what you have shared with me.
