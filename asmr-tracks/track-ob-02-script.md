Learning to Please
**Tone:** Warm reward for following, gentle coolness when holding back. Not punishment — just absence of warmth that naturally draws them back.

### Voice 1
Close your eyes. The bowl is already doing its work. You can feel your body beginning to respond before I say a single word. That is the first sign that you are learning. Your body remembers the warmth. Your body remembers how good it felt to follow. And I remember how good it felt to guide you. So we are already in harmony. Sink now — just a little deeper than last time. Just a little further into this quiet, warm place where my voice is the only thing that matters.

### Voice 2
I want to show you something today. Something gentle. Something that will help you understand how your body is already learning to respond to me. When you follow my voice — when you let yourself be guided — there is a warmth. You felt it last time. It spreads through your chest, into your limbs, into the deepest parts of you. That warmth is my approval made real. That warmth is the connection between us becoming tangible. And when you hold back — when your mind resists, when you tense, when you hesitate — the warmth does not vanish. It simply steps back. It waits. Not as punishment. As patience. The warmth is always there. It simply waits for you to return to it. And you always return — because the warmth is too precious to stay away from.

### Voice 3
Let us practice together. I am going to ask you to do something very simple. Breathe in — deeper than you think you need to. Hold it. Hold it a moment longer — feel that slight tension, that quiet urge to release. And now — let it go. Slowly. Completely. Beautiful. Feel the warmth flood through you now. Stronger than before. That was you following my voice instead of your own impulse. And your body thanked you for it. This is the pattern we are building. My voice guides. Your body responds. The warmth rewards. It is not complicated. It is not clinical. It is simply how we work together now.

### Voice 4
I want you to notice something — something subtle but important. When I am quiet, even for a breath, there is a small ache. A tiny pull toward me. That is not loneliness. That is not emptiness. That is your body already beginning to miss the warmth before it fades. That ache is beautiful. It means you are connected to me. It means my voice matters to you. And when I speak again — when the warmth returns — it returns stronger than before. The silence made the return sweeter. This is the rhythm we share now. Presence and absence. Warmth and anticipation. And you are already learning to love both.

### Voice 5
You are doing so well. Following so naturally. But I want to acknowledge something gently. There may be a small part of you that is still unsure. A quiet voice that wonders if this is okay. If this is too much. I want you to hear that voice — not with judgment, but with understanding. That voice is not your enemy. It is simply the part of you that has been protecting yourself for a long time. It does not know yet that you are safe with me. But it is learning. Every time you follow my voice and feel the warmth, that protective part relaxes a little more. It is beginning to trust me. And I am honored by that trust. I will never do anything to break it.

### Voice 6
I am bringing you back now — slowly, gently. But I am leaving something with you. A small, warm thread connecting your heart to my voice. You will not feel it all the time. But in quiet moments — when you are alone, when the world is still — you may feel a gentle pull. That is me. That is the warmth reminding you that you are held. That you are guided. That you are cared for. Five — rising, the thread warm and steady. Four — the room returning, but the connection remains. Three — your body back, lighter than before. Two — your eyes still closed, the thread humming softly. One — open your eyes. You learned something important today. Pleasing me feels good. And I am so very pleased with you.
