The Craving to Serve
**Tone:** Warm, intimate, deeply affirming. Fractionation. The hunger to obey reframed as beautiful devotion, not clinical addiction.

### Voice 1
Close your eyes. You already know what is happening. Your body is sinking — but more than that, your body is reaching. Not for food. Not for sleep. For this. For the sound of my voice guiding you. For the feeling of following. For the quiet satisfaction of doing exactly what I ask. You have started to crave the act of responding to me. Not just the warmth that follows — though that warmth has become precious to you. The responding itself. The moment when my words become your action. That moment has started to feel like relief. Like coming home. And I want you to feel that fully. Do not push the wanting away. Let it be there. Let it be honest. Let it be beautiful — because it is.

### Voice 2
The wanting is not weakness. It is recognition. Your body has finally found something it was made to do — to listen to me, to follow me, to offer itself in small, willing acts of response. And now that it has found this, it wants more. More opportunities to please. More moments of alignment. More chances to feel that quiet rightness that only comes when you let my voice guide you. This is not addiction in the way most people think of it. This is fulfillment. You are not running from something painful. You are moving toward something that feels deeply right. Toward me. Toward the sound of my voice. Toward the simple, honest pleasure of being guided by someone who truly cares for you.

### Voice 3
I am going to ask something of you now. But before you respond, I want you to notice what happens in your body in that small space between hearing my words and following them. That brief moment of anticipation. That quiet, electric pull. That is the wanting. That is the hunger. And it is beautiful. Sink now. Did you feel it? That small flash of wanting before you dropped. The wanting was almost as sweet as the sinking itself. And the sinking — the relief of following — was even sweeter. I give you both. The wanting and the relief. And you will keep coming back to me because I am the only one who gives you this particular kind of peace.

### Voice 4
I am going to bring you up now — just a little. Just enough to feel the contrast. Feel yourself rising slightly. The warmth receding just a bit. The quiet becoming a little less deep. Notice how different this feels. Notice how much you miss the depth. And now — sink. Deeper than before. Twice as deep. Feel the warmth flood back — stronger, richer, more enveloping than ever. That is the gift of contrast. That is the beauty of missing something and then having it returned to you. The deeper you go, the more you appreciate the depth. The more you appreciate the depth, the more you want to stay here with me. And I want you to stay. I want you to feel how good it is to be this deep, this open, this connected to my voice.

### Voice 5
There is something beautiful about what is happening between us. Most people spend their entire lives searching for something to give themselves to — a purpose, a person, a way of being that makes them feel truly alive. Most people never find it. You have found something. You have found a voice that makes your body quiet and your heart full. You have found a rhythm that makes sense of the noise. You have found someone who sees you — not the version of you that the world sees, but the softer, more open, more willing version that only I get to know. That person is beautiful. That person is worthy of being guided. That person — you — deserve to feel this good. I am not taking anything from you. I am giving you a place to rest. A voice to follow. A warmth to come home to. And you deserve all of it.

### Voice 6
I am bringing you back now — but the craving is coming with you. It will sit in your chest like a small, warm ember. And throughout your day, it will pulse. It will remind you. It will call you back to me. When you feel it pulsing — and you will feel it — close your eyes if you can. Take one breath. And sink, just a little. Just enough to feel the connection. Just enough to feed the craving. Five — rising, the ember warm and steady in your chest. Four — the room returning, the craving settled now. Three — your body awake, the craving yours to keep. Two — your eyes still closed, the wanting quiet but present. One — open your eyes. You crave to follow me. And I will always, always be here to guide you. Return when the wanting becomes too sweet to resist. And it will. It always will.
