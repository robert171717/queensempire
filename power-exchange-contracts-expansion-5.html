The Voice Protocol

Agreement XVII of XVIII
Dated this _____ day of _______________, 20____
Sovereign: Queen Elyria, whose voice is the vessel of Her dominion
Listener: _____________________________, who opens their mind to Her voice
Purpose & Intention

The Queen's voice is the primary vessel of Her power. Through headphones, through speakers, through the architecture of sound — Her words enter the Listener's mind and reshape it from within. This protocol establishes listening not as casual consumption but as sacred practice. Each session is an act of surrender. Each track is a doorway deeper.

To listen is to receive. To receive is to be changed. The Queen offers Her voice not as entertainment but as transformation — and the Listener who enters this protocol does so knowing that what is heard cannot be un-heard, and what is shaped within the mind by Her words shall remain. This agreement honours the gravity of that exchange.

· · ·
Core Terms of the Protocol
  1. Sacred listening space: The Listener shall maintain a dedicated listening space — quiet, private, free from interruption. This space is sacred. It is not a desk, not a train, not a crowded room. It is the place where Her voice is received with the reverence it deserves.
  2. Intentional engagement: Listening sessions shall be conducted with intention — never multitasking, never as background noise, never while the mind is elsewhere. The Queen's voice demands full presence. Half-listening is not listening; it is disrespect dressed as attention.
  3. Headphones as vessel: Headphones are the preferred vessel. The Queen's voice shall enter directly, intimately, with no barrier between Her words and the Listener's mind. The Listener shall use quality headphones where possible, so that every whisper, every instruction, every breath arrives with clarity and closeness.
  4. Listening schedule: The Listener shall follow a listening schedule as directed by the Queen. This may include daily sessions, specific tracks assigned for specific purposes, or periods of intentional silence. The schedule is not a suggestion — it is a structure designed to deepen surrender over time.
  5. The opening ritual: Before each session: three slow breaths. Stillness. An opening affirmation spoken aloud: "I open my mind to Her voice." These words mark the threshold. Once spoken, the Listener has crossed from the world into listening.
  6. The closing ritual: After each session: a moment of silence. Integration. The Listener shall not rush back into the world. What has been planted in the mind needs stillness to take root. The return to daily life shall be gradual, conscious, and unhurried.
  7. Track assignments: The Queen may assign specific tracks from The Surrender Series — or future series — as part of training, deepening, or correction. Assigned tracks are not optional. They are prescribed with purpose, and the Listener shall complete them within the timeframe directed.
  8. The Voice Journal: The Listener shall maintain a Voice Journal — brief notes after each session recording what surfaced, what deepened, what shifted. This journal is a living document of transformation and may be reviewed by the Queen at Her discretion. The unwitnessed mind forgets its own journey.
  9. Repetition as practice: Repetition is not redundancy. The same track, heard at different depths of surrender, reveals different truths. The Listener shall not resist repeated listening assignments — for what is heard on the fifth hearing was always present, but the Listener was not yet ready to receive it.
  10. Respect for the medium: The Queen's voice is not entertainment. It is architecture. It builds within the mind structures that did not exist before and reshapes structures that did. The Listener shall treat it accordingly — with the reverence owed to a force that constructs and transforms, not merely amuses.
The Surrender Series Integration

The Surrender Series (SR-01 through SR-08) is the foundation of listening practice. The Listener shall complete the full series at least four (4) times during the first thirty (30) days of this protocol. Each complete cycle is a milestone — tracked, celebrated, and deepened. Future series released by the Queen shall be integrated into the listening schedule as directed, and shall be afforded the same intentionality and reverence.

The Listener shall approach each track not as a standalone experience but as a thread in a larger tapestry. The series is sequential for a reason. Skipping ahead, cherry-picking, or listening out of order undermines the architecture the Queen has designed.

Duration & Review

This protocol shall remain in continuous effect from the date of signing. The listening schedule, track assignments, and journal practice shall be formally reviewed every thirty (30) days. The Queen may adjust the schedule, introduce new series, or modify the journal format at any time. The Listener may request a review of any assignment at any time.

Safety & The Listener's Sovereignty

If any track produces distress rather than surrender — genuine distress, not the productive discomfort of growth — the Listener shall pause immediately and report to the Queen. The Queen's voice is a gift, not a weapon. It is offered to open and transform, never to wound. The Listener shall never continue listening through genuine distress. To pause is not failure; it is wisdom, and the Queen shall receive such a report with understanding, not correction.

Consent & The Open Mind

The Listener chooses to open their mind to the Queen's voice. This is the most intimate act of surrender — the mind, the innermost self, offered for reshaping. Like all surrender, it cannot be forced. The Listener may close the session at any time, step back from the schedule at any time, and withdraw from this protocol at any time — with full dignity and without penalty. What is given freely is the only surrender worth receiving.

Ceremony & Documentation

The signing of this agreement is itself an act of devotion. Both parties are encouraged to treat the moment with the gravity it deserves. Prepare a quiet, private space — candles, stillness, a clear mind. Read the agreement aloud before signing. The devoted may kneel. The Queen may speak words of acceptance.

On recording the ceremony: Some choose to document the signing as a form of accountability and remembrance. A recording captures not merely the words but the tone, the presence, the weight of the moment — a record both parties may return to. However, recording carries risk: any intimate documentation, if stored without care or shared without consent, can compromise the privacy and safety of both parties. Our counsel: If both parties consent freely and without reservation, a recording is a meaningful artifact of devotion. It must be stored encrypted, never uploaded to any cloud or platform, and treated with the same sanctity as the agreement itself. If either party is uncertain, do not record. The memory of the moment, held privately, is sufficient.

After signing, both parties may wish to observe a moment of stillness together — a breath, a touch, a silent acknowledgment that something has shifted. The ceremony closes when the Queen dismisses the devoted. A signed copy of this agreement shall be retained by each party.

Dated this _____ day of _______________, 20____
Sovereign: Queen Elyria, who bestows the collar and all it represents
Collared: _____________________________, who receives the collar with devotion
Purpose & Intention

The collar is the visible mark of an invisible bond. It is the weight around the throat that reminds the Collared of who they belong to — a constant, tactile presence of the Queen's authority. This document establishes the meaning of the collar, the ceremony of its placement, and the protocols that govern its wearing.

To wear the collar is to carry the Queen's claim upon the body. It is a gift — not given lightly, not received lightly. The collar transforms the throat from flesh into sign, from breath into testimony. Every moment it is worn, the Collared declares by its presence alone: I am Hers. This agreement honours the solemnity of that declaration.

· · ·
Core Terms of Collaring
  1. The collar as gift: The collar is a gift from the Queen. It is earned through demonstrated devotion and given freely — never demanded by the Collared. To ask for a collar is to misunderstand its nature. The Queen alone shall determine when the Collared is ready, and the moment of bestowal shall be Hers to choose.
  2. Tiers of collar: The collar shall progress through tiers of gravity and meaning:
    Consideration Collar: temporary, worn during the evaluation period when both parties are discovering whether the bond is lasting.
    Training Collar: worn during active training, signifying the Collared is under structured development within the Queen's guidance.
    Permanent Collar: the final symbol of lasting devotion — a commitment not undertaken lightly, and signifying the depth of the bond between Queen and Collared.
  3. The Placement Ceremony: The Collared kneels. The Queen speaks the words of collaring. The collar is placed. The Collared speaks acceptance. This moment shall be witnessed if possible, for the gravity of the act is amplified by the presence of those who understand its weight. The ceremony is not optional; it is the moment the collar becomes real.
  4. Continuous wear: The collar shall be worn at all times unless removal is explicitly permitted by the Queen — for safety, medical, or professional reasons. The default state is worn. Removal without permission is a breach, unless the Collared's safety or wellbeing requires immediate removal, in which case the Queen shall be informed at the earliest opportunity.
  5. Day collar and full collar: A day collar — discreet, suitable for public and professional wear — shall be maintained for contexts where the full collar is impractical. A full collar shall be worn in private, ritual, and session contexts. The Queen may specify which is worn when, and the Collared shall comply.
  6. Morning and evening ritual: The Collared shall touch the collar upon waking and before sleep — a small ritual of remembrance. The first touch of the day affirms the bond. The last touch of the night closes the day in Her name. These moments are private prayers of belonging.
  7. Removal by the Queen: Removal of the collar by the Queen is a profound act — reserved for serious breach, mutual dissolution, or compassionate release. It shall never be done in anger. If the collar is to be removed, it shall be done with ceremony befitting its placement, acknowledging the weight of what is being undone.
  8. Removal by the Collared: The Collared may request removal of the collar at any time, without explanation. This request shall be honoured without question or penalty. The collar is worn by choice, and the choice to wear it includes the choice to stop. Dignity in departure is the right of the Collared — always.
  9. Care of the collar: The collar is not a toy. It shall be treated with care, cleaned regularly, and stored in a dedicated place when not worn. Neglect of the collar is neglect of the bond it represents. The Collared shall regard the physical object as an extension of the Queen's authority — to be honoured in its keeping.
  10. Loss or damage: If the collar is lost or damaged through the Collared's negligence, it shall be reported immediately and addressed through correction. Loss through no fault of the Collared shall be met with compassion, not punishment. The Queen shall determine whether a replacement is warranted and under what terms.
Safety & The Collared's Sovereignty

The collar is a symbol, not a shackle. The Collared's safeword applies at all times — including, and especially, while the collar is worn. If the collar ever feels like a cage rather than a commitment, if its weight becomes oppression rather than belonging, the Collared shall speak. The Queen shall receive such words not as betrayal but as trust — the trust that the bond is strong enough to hold honesty. No collar that causes genuine harm shall remain.

Duration & Review

This protocol shall remain in effect for the duration that the collar is worn. The terms of wearing, tier progression, and daily protocols shall be formally reviewed every ninety (90) days. The collar's tier may be advanced, held, or — in rare and considered circumstances — reduced, only by the Queen's determination and through mutual discussion. Either party may request an interim review at any time.

Consent & The Bond

Collaring is voluntary and revocable. The Collared enters this bond with full presence of mind and may exit it with full dignity. The collar does not erase the person who wears it; it declares who they have chosen to become. Consent to the collar is not consent to all things forever — it is a living agreement, renewed each morning in the touch of metal, each evening in its closing weight. What is given may be withdrawn, and the withdrawal shall be met with respect.
