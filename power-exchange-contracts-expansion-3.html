Aftercare Covenant

Agreement XIII of XIV
Dated this _____ day of _______________, 20____
Guardian: Queen Elyria, who holds the sacred duty of care
Recipient: _____________________________, who receives care after surrender
Purpose & Intention

This covenant recognises that what follows a scene is as sacred as the scene itself. Aftercare is not an addendum, not a courtesy, and not a reward — it is the bridge between surrender and the world, the space where the devoted is reassembled after being unmade. When the body has been pushed and the mind has opened, the return to wholeness is not automatic. It must be tended.

The Guardian enters into this covenant acknowledging that the duty of care is inseparable from the exercise of power. To hold someone open is to hold responsibility for their closing. This agreement formalises the protocols, rhythms, and obligations that shall govern the care of the Recipient after every act of power exchange.

· · ·
Core Terms of Aftercare
  1. Immediate commencement: Aftercare shall begin the instant any scene, session, ritual, or act of power exchange concludes — without exception, without delay, without negotiation at the moment. The transition from intensity to care is not a gap; it is a threshold, and it shall be crossed deliberately.
  2. Physical aftercare shall include provision of water, warmth (blanket or body heat), comfort, first aid if required, and access to a quiet, safe space in which the Recipient may rest and recover. The body that was offered shall be honoured in its returning.
  3. Emotional aftercare shall include verbal reassurance, affirmation of worth and value, and gentle reconnection to the self — the name, the body, the present moment — bringing the Recipient back from the deep places with tenderness and patience. The mind that was opened shall be guided gently home.
  4. The Guardian shall remain physically or emotionally present for a minimum of thirty (30) minutes following any session, and longer as the Recipient's state requires. Presence is not passive waiting; it is active, attentive care.
  5. A check-in shall occur within twenty-four (24) hours following any intense session. This is not optional. This is not a courtesy. It is a duty written into the architecture of the dynamic — a confirmation that the Recipient has returned to steady ground.
  6. Sub-drop protocol: Both parties shall be educated in the recognition of sub-drop — the emotional and physiological crash that may follow deep surrender. The signs shall be known to both. An escalation procedure shall be agreed. An emergency contact shall be on file. What goes up must be caught on the way down.
  7. The Recipient's aftercare needs may evolve over time. A review of aftercare preferences, known triggers, and desired forms of care shall be conducted every thirty (30) days, ensuring the protocols remain attuned to the Recipient's changing landscape.
  8. Aftercare is not a reward for good behaviour. It is not earned through compliance. It is not withheld as correction. It is an inalienable right of the one who surrenders — inseparable from the act of surrender itself. To treat aftercare as conditional is to misunderstand the nature of the exchange.
  9. Should the Guardian be unavailable for aftercare — due to distance, scheduling, or circumstance — a surrogate aftercare protocol shall be agreed in advance, naming who shall provide care, in what form, and by what method. No scene shall conclude without a plan for the care that follows.
  10. Neglect or refusal of aftercare by the Guardian is a breach of the highest order — a violation of the trust upon which the entire dynamic rests. Such neglect shall be treated with the utmost seriousness and may constitute grounds for the dissolution of the dynamic itself.
Duration & Review

This covenant shall remain in continuous effect, active after every session, ritual, or act of power exchange without exception. The aftercare protocols shall be formally reviewed every thirty (30) days and updated as the Recipient's needs evolve. Either party may request an interim review at any time.

Consent & The Right to Care

Aftercare is non-negotiable. The Recipient may request additional care, additional time, or additional presence at any time — without shame, without penalty, without fear that such a request reflects weakness or failure. To need care is human. To ask for it is courage. The Guardian shall receive all such requests with openness and grace, understanding that the measure of a Guardian is not in the power exercised during a scene, but in the tenderness offered after.

Ceremony & Documentation

The signing of this agreement is itself an act of devotion. Both parties are encouraged to treat the moment with the gravity it deserves. Prepare a quiet, private space — candles, stillness, a clear mind. Read the agreement aloud before signing. The devoted may kneel. The Queen may speak words of acceptance.

On recording the ceremony: Some choose to document the signing as a form of accountability and remembrance. A recording captures not merely the words but the tone, the presence, the weight of the moment — a record both parties may return to. However, recording carries risk: any intimate documentation, if stored without care or shared without consent, can compromise the privacy and safety of both parties. Our counsel: If both parties consent freely and without reservation, a recording is a meaningful artifact of devotion. It must be stored encrypted, never uploaded to any cloud or platform, and treated with the same sanctity as the agreement itself. If either party is uncertain, do not record. The memory of the moment, held privately, is sufficient.

After signing, both parties may wish to observe a moment of stillness together — a breath, a touch, a silent acknowledgment that something has shifted. The ceremony closes when the Queen dismisses the devoted. A signed copy of this agreement shall be retained by each party.

Discipline & Correction Codex

Agreement XIV of XIV
Dated this _____ day of _______________, 20____
Sovereign: Queen Elyria, who alone determines when and how correction is given
Subject: _____________________________, who receives correction with grace and purpose
Purpose & Intention

This codex exists to bring intention, structure, and integrity to the act of correction within the dynamic. Discipline is not anger given an outlet. Correction is not cruelty dressed in protocol. The Queen shapes behaviour, corrects shortcomings, and reinforces devotion — always with purpose, always with proportion, always with the growth of the Subject at the centre of Her intent.

When correction is administered with clarity and care, it deepens the bond between Sovereign and Subject. When it is administered in anger or without intention, it erodes that bond. This codex draws the line between the two with precision, so that both parties may trust the framework within which correction is given and received.

· · ·
Core Terms of Correction
  1. Sovereign authority: The Queen alone shall determine when correction is warranted, what form it shall take, and when it is concluded. The Subject does not invite, negotiate, or contest the fact of correction — though the Subject retains the absolute right to pause or halt correction through safeword at any time, for any reason.
  2. Correction tiers shall ascend in gravity according to the nature of the shortcoming:
    Level I — Verbal Redirection: a spoken correction, clear and direct, naming the shortcoming and the expectation.
    Level II — Restriction of Privilege: the temporary withdrawal of a granted privilege as correction, proportionate and time-bound.
    Level III — Physical Discipline: administered within agreed limits, proportionate and purposeful, never in haste.
    Level IV — Ceremonial Correction: a formal, ritualised session reserved for matters of significant gravity, conducted with the full weight of protocol.
  3. Principle of proportion: All correction shall be proportionate to the shortcoming. A small failure does not warrant a great correction. Correction is teaching, not venting. The Queen shall calibrate the response to the infraction, always.
  4. Grace in reception: The Subject shall receive all correction with grace — without argument, without deflection, without sulking — acknowledging that correction serves the dynamic and the Subject's growth within it. Resistance to the process undermines its purpose.
  5. Words of acceptance: Following the administration of correction, the Subject shall speak words of acceptance and gratitude — not for the correction itself, but for the attention, care, and investment in the Subject's development that it represents. The matter, once accepted, is closed.
  6. The anger prohibition: No correction shall be administered while the Queen is in genuine anger. If anger is present, the Queen shall pause, regain composure, and address the matter once She is centred. Correction given in anger is not correction; it is retaliation, and it has no place in this codex.
  7. Correction journal: A correction journal shall be maintained — recording the date, the infraction, the correction administered, and the outcome. This journal is a living document of growth, available for review by both parties, and shall never be used as a weapon of shame.
  8. Quarterly pattern review: A review of all recorded corrections shall be conducted every quarter to assess patterns — recurring infractions, the effectiveness of correction methods, and any adjustments to protocols that may better serve the Subject's development.
  9. Safeword protections: Safeword protections shall apply during all correction, without exception. "Amber" shall pause the correction for discussion and recalibration. "Crimson" shall halt correction entirely and immediately. The Subject's safeword is sacred and shall never be held against the Subject, nor cited as further infraction.
  10. Post-correction processing: The Subject may request a post-correction discussion no sooner than twenty-four (24) hours following the correction — a space to process, ask questions, seek understanding, and close the matter with clarity. The Queen shall honour such requests in good faith.
Duration & Review

This codex shall remain in continuous effect from the date of signing and shall be formally reviewed every sixty (60) days. The correction tiers, protocols, and journal may be amended by mutual agreement during these reviews. Either party may request an interim review at any time.

Safety & The Boundary of Correction

Correction is for growth, never for abuse. The Subject retains the absolute, non-negotiable right to refuse any correction that feels unsafe — physically, emotionally, or psychologically. A safeword spoken is a correction refused, and it shall be honoured immediately and without question. The Queen governs correction; She does not govern the Subject's right to safety. These are separate domains, and neither encroaches upon the other.
